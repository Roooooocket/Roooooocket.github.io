Microwave-assisted One-pot Synthesis of Carbon-dots for Highly Sensitive and Selective Detection of Selenite

Role 3rd Author
Journal Microchemical Journal (IF: 4.821, Q2)
Year 2022
DOI 10.1016/j.microc.2022.107440

Research Background

Selenium (Se) is an essential micronutrient important for human health, playing roles as antioxidant and immunoregulatory agent. However, only a narrow range of selenium concentrations is beneficial — the recommended intake for adults is 55 μg/day, with a tolerable upper limit of 400 μg/day.

Excessive selenium intake can be toxic, leading to oxidative stress. The toxicity depends on chemical form — Se(IV) (selenite) is much more harmful than other forms. Therefore, accurate and sensitive determination of Se(IV) in water resources is crucial for human health.

🔬 Research Problem

Traditional methods for selenium detection require expensive instruments, professional operators, and complex procedures with poor时效性 (time efficiency). We needed a simple, fast, and sensitive method.

Methodology

We developed a label-free, sensitive fluorescence method using carbon dots (CDs) synthesized via microwave-assisted pyrolysis. The synthesis is remarkably simple and fast:

  • Citric Acid + DAB dissolved in water
  • Microwave heating: 4 minutes
  • Uniform size: ~2.6 nm
  • Maximum excitation: 400 nm, Emission: 450 nm
  • Quantum yield: ~8%
