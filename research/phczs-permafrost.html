Cold Trapping and Frost Filter Effects on Polyhalogenated Carbazoles in Tibetan Plateau Permafrost

Role Lead Researcher
Institution RCEES, Chinese Academy of Sciences
Period 2024 – Present
Status Manuscript in Preparation

Research Background

The Tibetan Plateau, known as the "Third Pole," is highly sensitive to long-range atmospheric transport (LRAT) of organic pollutants. Its extreme altitude and cold climate create unique environmental conditions for studying pollutant deposition.

Polyhalogenated carbazoles (PHCZs) are emerging organic pollutants with potential ecological and health risks. Understanding their behavior in remote high-altitude environments is crucial for assessing their global distribution and fate.

🎯 Research Focus

This project investigates cold trapping and frost filter effects on PHCZs across permafrost, vegetation, and elevational gradients in the Tibetan Plateau, at altitudes exceeding 5,000 meters.

Research Questions

Question 1: Spatial Distribution

How do PHCZ concentrations vary across different elevations and environmental matrices (permafrost, soil, vegetation) on the Tibetan Plateau?

Question 2: Cold Trapping Effect

What is the role of mountain cold trapping in the accumulation of PHCZs in high-altitude permafrost environments?

Question 3: Frost Filter Mechanism

How does the seasonal freeze-thaw cycle act as a "frost filter" affecting PHCZ deposition and transformation?

Field Work

Leading high-altitude fieldwork (>5,000 m) on the Tibetan Plateau, involving:

  • Permafrost core drilling and sampling
  • Vegetation sample collection across elevational gradients
  • Soil profile sampling at multiple depths
  • GPS coordinate recording and environmental parameter monitoring

High-altitude permafrost core drilling

Plant samples across elevational gradient

Analytical Methods

Comprehensive chemical analysis using state-of-the-art instrumentation:

  • GC-MS: PHCZ identification and quantification
  • Structural Equation Modeling (SEM): Statistical analysis of environmental drivers
  • Radionuclide dating: Age dating of permafrost cores
  • Multivariate statistics: Source apportionment and trend analysis

Skills & Expertise

Field Sampling Permafrost Core Drilling High-altitude Research GC-MS Analysis Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) Environmental Statistics Source Apportionment Scientific Writing

Expected Contributions

This research will provide novel insights into:

  • The behavior of emerging organic pollutants in climate-sensitive environments
  • The mechanism of cold trapping for halogenated compounds at high altitudes
  • The frost filter effect on pollutant deposition and cycling
  • Baseline data for future monitoring of PHCZs in the Tibetan Plateau region
